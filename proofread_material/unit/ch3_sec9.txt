\section{Examples of Logical Principles}\label{sec:validExamplesBis}

This section collects together some logical equivalences and
logical implications that pertain to quantifiers.
Some of the results below have been proven already. For many of the others,
see Exercises \ref{exer:startQidentities}-\ref{exer:endQidentities}.

\paragraph*{Some logical equivalences for quantifiers.}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c@{~~~-~}l}
$\forall x\, \forall y\, A \tautEq \forall y\, \forall x\, A$ & Quantifier exchange \\
$\exists x\, \exists y\, A \tautEq \exists y\, \exists x\, A$ & Quantifier exchange \\
$\forall x\, (A \land B) \tautEq \forall x\, A \land \forall x\, B$ & Distributing $\forall$ over $\land$ \\
$\exists x\, (A \lor B) \tautEq \exists x\, A \lor \exists x\, B$ & Distributing $\exists$ over $\lor$ \\
$\exists x\, (A \limplies B) \tautEq \forall x\, A \limplies \exists x\, B$ & Distributing $\exists$ over $\limplies$ \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph*{Some logical implications for quantifiers.}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{l@{~~~}l}
$\vDash \forall x\, A \limplies \exists x\, A$ & -~Corollary \ref{coro:AimpliesE} \\
$\vDash \exists x\, \forall y\, A \limplies \forall y\, \exists x\, A$ &  \\
$\vDash \forall x\, A \limplies A$ \\
$\vDash \forall x\, A \limplies A(t/x)$ & -~If $t$ is substitutable for $x$ in $A$ \\
$\vDash A \limplies \exists x\, A$ & \\
$\vDash A(t/x) \limplies \exists x\, A$ & -~If $t$ is substitutable for $x$ in $A$ \\
$\vDash \forall x\, (A \limplies B) \limplies \exists x\, A \limplies \exists x\, B$ & \\
$\vDash \forall x\, (A \limplies B) \limplies \forall x\, A \limplies \forall x\, B$ & \\
$\vDash \forall x\, \forall y\, A \limplies \forall x \, A(x/y)$ & -~If $x$ is substitutable for~$y$ in $A$.
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The final logical validity may be a little confusing;
it can be expressed more clearly with the relaxed notation
for substitution as
\[
\vDash \forall x\, \forall y\, A(x,y) \limplies \forall x\, A(x,x) .
\]
The implications above in general do not reverse; for instance,
$\vDash A \limplies \forall x \, A$ is not true in general.
They do reverse in some special cases, notably when the quantifiers
are ``vacuous''\index{vacuous quantifier} and do not actually bind any variables.
For instance, the next two equivalences follow from
Exercise~\ref{exer:QxNotFree}.
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{l@{~~~}l}
$\vDash A \liff \forall x\, A $ \qquad  & -~If $x$ does not occur free in~$A$ \\
$\vDash \exists x\, A \liff A$ & -~If $x$ does not occur free in~$A$ \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph*{Logical equivalences supporting prenex form.}

In all of the below formulas, it is assumed that $x$ does not
have any free occurrences in~$C$. Other than the first four equivalences,
these are stated in Lemma~\ref{lem:prenexHelpers}.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c@{~~~}l}
$\forall x\, A \tautEq \lnot \exists x\, \lnot A$ & -~Theorem \ref{thm:negQneg} \\
$\exists x\, A \tautEq \lnot \forall x\, \lnot A$ & -~\hspace*{2em}$''$ \\
$\lnot \forall x\, A \tautEq \exists x\, \lnot A$ & -~Theorem \ref{thm:negQnegBis} \\
$\lnot \exists x\, A \tautEq \forall x\, \lnot A$ & -~\hspace*{2em}$''$  \\
$C \land \exists x\, A \tautEq \exists x\,(C \land A)$  \\
$C \land \forall x\, A \tautEq \forall x\,(C \land A)$ & \\
$C \lor \forall x\, A \tautEq \forall x\,(C \lor A)$ & \\
$C \lor \exists x\, A \tautEq \exists x\,(C \lor A)$ & \\
$C \limplies \forall x\, A \tautEq \forall x\,(C \limplies A)$ & -~Example \ref{example:prenexACI} \\
$C \limplies \exists x\, A \tautEq \exists x\,(C \limplies A)$ & \\
$(\forall x\, A) \limplies C \tautEq \exists x\,(A \limplies C)$ & \\
$(\exists x\, A) \limplies C \tautEq \forall x\,(A \limplies C)$ & \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\noindent
To reiterate: it is assumed that $x$ is not free in~$C$
in the above logical equivalences.
