\section{Principles of Equality}\label{sec:equality}\index{equality}

We now discuss several principles of equality.
The basic underlying philosophy is that if objects
are equal, then they should satisfy the same properties.
The first principles state that equality~($=$) defines an equivalence
relation:

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:EQ123instances}
Let $r$, $s$ and~$t$ be terms.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\vDash t=t$.  {\rm  (Reflexivity)}\index{reflexivity}
\item $\vDash s=t \limplies t=s$.  {\rm  (Symmetry)}\index{symmetry}
\item $\vDash r=s \limplies s=t \limplies r=t$.  {\rm  (Transitivity)}\index{transitivity}
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
These logical validities follow immediately from the definition
of truth. For instance, transitivity follows from the fact that
if an object assignment~$\sigma$ satisfies $\sigma(r)=\sigma(s)$
and $\sigma(s)=\sigma(t)$, then $\sigma(r)=\sigma(t)$.
\end{proof}

The next principles state that predicate and function symbols
respect equality.  These were already stated and proved
in Lemma~\ref{lem:equalityAxioms}, and are restated here for convenience:
\[
\vDash ~ \bigand_{i=1}^k s_i = t_i ~\limplies~ [ P(s_1,\ldots, s_k) \liff P(t_1,\ldots t_k)].
\]
and
\[
\vDash ~ \bigand_{i=1}^k s_i = t_i ~\limplies~  f(s_1,\ldots, s_k) = f(t_1,\ldots t_k).
\]
A more general version of the equality principle holds for arbitrary formulas~$A$ in place of
a predicate~$P$:
\begin{theorem}\label{thm:equalityPrincipleSemantic}
Let $A(x_{i_1},\ldots, x_{i_k})$ be a formula
and let $s_1, \ldots, s_k$ and $t_1,\ldots, t_k$ be terms. Suppose
that each $s_j$ and $t_j$ is substitutable for $x_{i_j}$
in~$A$.
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:equalityPrincipleSemanticThm}
\vDash ~ \bigand_{i=1}^k s_i = t_i ~\limplies~ [ A(s_1,\ldots, s_k) \liff A(t_1,\ldots t_k)].
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Without loss of generality, the variables $x_{i_1}\ldots,x_{i_k}$ do not
appear in the terms $s_1,\ldots, s_k$ and $t_1,\ldots, t_k$, since otherwise
the variables~$x_{i_j}$ can be renamed in~$A$.
Equation~(\ref{eq:equalityAxiomsThm}) of Theorem~\ref{thm:substitution}
gives
\[
\vDash ~ \bigand_{i=1}^\ell x_{i_j} = t_j ~\limplies~  [ A(\vec x) \liff A(\vec t)].
\]
From this, Corollary~\ref{coro:UIfreevariable} gives
(\ref{eq:equalityPrincipleSemanticThm}) as desired.
\end{proof}
